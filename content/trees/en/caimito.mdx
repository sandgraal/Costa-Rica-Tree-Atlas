## Physical Description

### Overall Form

The Caimito is a handsome evergreen tree with a dense, spreading crown. Its most distinctive feature is the leaves: dark glossy green above but covered with **satiny golden-bronze pubescence** below. When the wind moves through the canopy, the leaves flash gold and greenâ€”a truly stunning sight. The tree produces abundant round fruits and provides excellent shade.

## The Star Pattern

### Why It's Called Star Apple

<FeatureBox title="The Secret Star Inside" icon="â­">
  When you cut a ripe Caimito fruit **horizontally** (crosswise), you reveal a
  beautiful natural star: **What You See**: - The seeds are arranged around the
  center - Translucent pulp segments radiate outward - The pattern forms a 5-8
  pointed star - Purple varieties: star shows purple and white - Green
  varieties: star shows greenish and white **Perfect for Instagram**: The
  cross-section makes for stunning food photography! **Cultural Significance**:
  In many cultures, the star is considered auspicious, and Caimitos are
  associated with good fortune.
</FeatureBox>

  <AccordionItem title="Climate Requirements">

<AccordionItem title="Propagation">
  **From Seed**: - Fresh seeds germinate in 3-4 weeks - Seedlings variable in
  quality - Takes 5-10 years to fruit **Vegetative** (preferred): - Air layering
  common - Grafting for named varieties - Fruiting in 3-4 years - Ensures fruit

  <AccordionItem title="Seasonal Care Calendar">
    ### December-February (Dry Season - Veranillo)
    - Regular watering for young trees (0-5 years): weekly deep watering
    - Mature trees: biweekly if prolonged dry spell
    - Monitor for water stress (leaf wilting indicates need)
    - Fertilize with balanced formula (10-10-10) + micronutrients
    - **Fruiting varies by variety**: Some varieties fruit now

    ### March-April (Pre-Rainy Season)
    - **Peak flowering for many varieties**: August-October flowers set fruit now
    - Fruit development begins (3-4 month maturation)
    - Reduce irrigation gradually as rains begin
    - Apply compost/organic matter around drip line
    - Light pruning after fruiting (shape young trees)

    ### May-August (Rainy Season - Invierno)
    - Natural rainfall sufficient - cease irrigation
    - **Peak fruiting period: May-June** for many varieties
    - Daily harvest checks - fruits soften when ripe
    - Monitor for fruit rot (rare, good drainage prevents)
    - Weed management important (vigorous rainy season growth)

    ### September-November (Late Rainy/Early Dry)
    - **Flowering begins**: August-October peak flowering for many varieties
    - Late-season fruits ripen
    - Propagation window: Air layering success highest now
    - Prepare for dry season (mulch application)
    - Assess tree health, plan any corrective pruning

    **Critical Notes**:
    - Caimito flowers/fruits on old wood - avoid heavy pruning
    - Fruit does NOT ripen after picking - must be mature when harvested
    - Color change + slight softness = harvest indicator
    - Grafted trees fruit 3-4 years; seedlings 5-10+ years
    - Despite common name "Star Apple", fruits cause no allergies (unlike true apples)
    - Golden leaf undersides make tree ornamental even when not fruiting
