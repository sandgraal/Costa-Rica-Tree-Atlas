# Aceituno

<Callout type="success" title="The Paradise Tree">
  The **Aceituno** (_Simarouba amara_), also known as **Paradise Tree** or
  **Bitterwood**, is a remarkable fast-growing rainforest tree valued across the
  Americas for its potent medicinal properties, lightweight wood, and oil-rich
  seeds. Indigenous peoples have used its bitter bark for centuries to treat
  fevers, parasites, and digestive ailments.
</Callout>

<AccordionItem title="ðŸ”¬ Taxonomic Notes">

The family Simaroubaceae is known for producing bitter compounds (quassinoids) used medicinally. The genus name _Simarouba_ comes from a Galibi (Caribbean indigenous) word for the tree, while _amara_ means "bitter" in Latinâ€”referring to the intensely bitter bark.

**Medicinal Family**: The Simaroubaceae includes other famous medicinal plants like Quassia (_Quassia amara_) and Tree of Heaven (_Ailanthus altissima_).

**Chemical Profile**: The bitter compounds that make the bark medicinally valuable also make the wood naturally resistant to termites and other insects.
