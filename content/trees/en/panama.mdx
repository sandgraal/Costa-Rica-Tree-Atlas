<AccordionItem title="üî¨ Taxonomic Notes">

The genus _Sterculia_ was formerly placed in its own family (Sterculiaceae), now merged into Malvaceae. The name derives from Sterculius, Roman god of manure, referring to the unpleasant smell of some species' flowers.

**Historical Significance**: Legend has it that the Republic of Panama was named after this tree, which was abundant in the area where Panama City was founded.

**Former Classification**: _Sterculia apetala_ (Aubl.) H.Karst. ‚Äî The species name "apetala" means "without petals," referring to the apetalous flowers.
