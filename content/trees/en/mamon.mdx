### General Form

The Mam贸n is a handsome, medium to large tree with a dense, rounded crown that provides excellent shade. It's slow-growing but long-lived, eventually becoming a substantial presence in the landscape. The tree is semi-deciduous, losing leaves briefly during the dry season.

### Identification Features

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Bark and Trunk

- **Bark color**: Gray to grayish-brown
- **Texture**: Smooth when young, fissured with age
- **Trunk**: Straight, well-formed
- **Buttresses**: Sometimes present at base

#### Leaves

- **Type**: Compound, paripinnate
- **Leaflets**: Usually 4 (2 pairs), rarely 6
- **Size**: Leaflets 5-10 cm long
- **Shape**: Elliptic to lanceolate
- **Color**: Dark green, glossy
- **Arrangement**: Alternate
- **Notable**: Winged rachis (leaf stem)

</Column>
<Column>

#### Flowers

- **Size**: Small (4-5 mm)
- **Color**: Greenish-white
- **Arrangement**: Terminal and axillary panicles
- **Fragrance**: Sweet, honey-like
- **Timing**: Late dry season
- **Sexes**: Male and female usually on separate trees

#### Fruit

- **Shape**: Round to slightly oval
- **Size**: 2-3.5 cm diameter
- **Skin**: Green, thin, brittle
- **Flesh**: Translucent salmon-pink, gelatinous
- **Seed**: Large, single, fills most of fruit
- **Taste**: Sweet-tart, grape-like
- **Arrangement**: In grape-like clusters

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

<Callout type="tip" title="How to Eat Mam贸n">
  **The proper technique**:
  1. Crack the thin shell with your teeth
  2. Pop the fruit in your mouth
  3. SUCK the gelatinous flesh off the large seed
  4. Work the seed with your tongue to get all the pulp
  5. Discard seed (or save for planting!)

**Warning**: The flesh stains! The slightly astringent pulp can leave marks on clothing. Eating mam贸n is best done as street food, not at formal occasions.

</Callout>

## Cultivation

### Growing Mam贸n

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Site Selection">
    **Ideal Conditions**:
    - Full sun
    - Deep, well-drained soil
    - Space for large tree (15+ m spread)
    - Protection from strong winds when young
    - Lowland to mid-elevation

    **Considerations**:
    - Slow growthpatience required
    - Both male and female trees needed for fruit
    - Fruit drop can be messy
    - Long-lived investment (50+ years)

  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Propagation">
    **From Seed** (most common):
    - Use fresh seeds immediately
    - Plant 2-3 cm deep
    - Germination in 1-3 weeks
    - Slow early growth
    - Fruit in 8-10 years
    - Sex unknown until flowering

    **Air Layering** (for known sex):
    - Allows selecting female trees
    - Faster fruiting (4-5 years)
    - Maintains parent characteristics

    **Grafting**:
    - For superior varieties
    - Ensures female trees
    - Difficult but possible

  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Care">
    **Watering**: Regular when young, drought-tolerant when mature

    **Fertilization**: Light fertilization in growing season

    **Pruning**: Minimal needed; shape when young

    **Pest/Disease**: Generally problem-free

    **Harvest**: When fruit easily detaches from cluster

  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>
