# Jobo (Yellow Mombin / Hog Plum)

<Callout type="success" title="The Original Tropical Fruit">
  The **Jobo** (_Spondias mombin_), also called **Yellow Mombin** or **Hog
  Plum**, is one of the tropics' most beloved fruit trees. Its **tart, aromatic
  yellow plums** have been enjoyed by humans for thousands of years, and its
  remarkable ability to grow from large stem cuttings makes it the backbone of
  living fence systems across Central America. Every part of this tree is
  usefulfrom its refreshing fruits to its massive branches that take root when
  simply stuck in the ground.
</Callout>

## Physical Description

### General Form

The Jobo is a large deciduous tree with a spreading crown and thick, often crooked trunk. The bark is distinctiverough, thick, and deeply furrowed. The tree drops its leaves during the dry season, often fruiting while leafless or as new leaves emerge. Its ability to grow from large stem cuttings makes it unique among tropical trees.

### Distinctive Features

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Leaves

- **Type**: Pinnately compound
- **Leaflets**: 9-19 per leaf
- **Size**: 5-10 cm per leaflet
- **Arrangement**: Alternate, opposite leaflets
- **Feature**: Crushed leaves aromatic
- **Deciduous**: Drops leaves in dry season

#### Bark

- **Color**: Gray-brown
- **Texture**: Deeply furrowed, rough
- **Thickness**: Thick, corky
- **Inner Bark**: Reddish, aromatic
- **Exudate**: Resinous sap

</Column>
<Column>

#### The Beloved Fruit

- **Type**: Drupe (stone fruit)
- **Shape**: Ovoid, plum-like
- **Size**: 3-5 cm long
- **Color**: Yellow to orange when ripe
- **Skin**: Thin, slightly waxy
- **Flesh**: Yellow, juicy, very tart
- **Pit**: Large, fibrous stone
- **Season**: Late dry/early wet season

#### Flowers

- **Type**: Small, cream-white
- **Size**: 5-6 mm
- **Arrangement**: Terminal panicles
- **Timing**: Before or with new leaves
- **Fragrance**: Sweet, honey-like
- **Pollinators**: Bees, flies, wasps

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

<Callout type="tip" title="The Unmistakable Jobo Flavor">
  Jobo fruit has a distinctive taste profile: - **Dominant Note**: Intensely
  tart/sour - **Secondary**: Sweet undertones - **Aroma**: Fruity, mildly
  resinous - **Texture**: Juicy, fibrous near pit - **Aftertaste**: Slightly
  astringent The tartness means many people prefer Jobo in: - Drinks (frescos,
  licuados) - With salt (cuts tartness) - Preserves and jams - Dried with sugar
  Eating one fresh is an... intense experience!
</Callout>

## The Living Fence Tradition

### Stakes That Become Trees

<FeatureBox title="The Amazing Living Fence" icon="">
  One of Jobo's most remarkable characteristics is its ability to root from
  large stem cuttings: **The Process**: 1. Cut large branches (5-20 cm diameter,
  2-3 m long) 2. Simply stick cut end into the ground 3. Branch produces roots
  within weeks 4. Develops into a full tree over years 5. Wire can be attached
  for fencing **Benefits**: - No need to buy fence posts - Posts don't rot or
  need replacement - Trees provide shade for cattle - Fruit bonus for farmer and
  livestock - Carbon sequestration - Wildlife corridors This traditional
  technology has been used for centuries!
</FeatureBox>

### Living Fence Management

<DataTable
  headers={["Aspect", "Practice", "Benefits"]}

<Callout type="success" title="Sustainable Agriculture in Action">
  Living fences of Jobo represent traditional knowledge at its finest: -
  **Economic**: Free fencing material forever - **Ecological**: Habitat
  connectivity, carbon storage - **Practical**: Shade improves livestock welfare
  - **Bonus**: Free fruit crop - **Resilient**: Self-repairing, storm-resistant
  Modern agroforestry programs actively promote this ancient practice!

## Ecological Importance

### Wildlife Food Source

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Mammal Consumers

- **Peccaries**: Love fallen fruits
- **Deer**: Eat fruits in dry season
- **Monkeys**: Major consumers
- **Bats**: Eat ripe fruit, disperse seeds
- **Agoutis**: Cache and consume
- **Wild Pigs**: Hence "hog plum"!

</Column>
<Column>

#### Bird Interactions

- **Parrots**: Eat unripe and ripe fruits
- **Toucans**: Swallow fruits whole
- **Chachalacas**: Ground foragers
- **Tanagers**: Peck at fruits
- **Many more**: Major food tree

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

### Forest Dynamics

<Callout type="info" title="Ecosystem Services">
  The Jobo provides multiple ecosystem services: - **Food Web**: Critical fruit
  during dry season - **Seed Dispersal**: Animals carry seeds widely -
  **Shade**: Improves microclimate in pastures - **Pollination**: Flowers
  support many insects - **Connectivity**: Living fences link forest fragments
  Removing Jobo trees from the landscape would impact numerous species!
</Callout>

## Cultivation

### Growing from Cuttings

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Selecting and Preparing Stakes">
    **Best Material**: - Branch diameter: 5-20 cm - Length: 2-3 meters - Age:
    Mature wood (not green) - Condition: Healthy, no disease **Preparation**: -
    Cut at an angle (bottom end) - Allow to dry for a few days - Remove leaves -
    Plant within 1-2 weeks
  </AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="Planting">
  **Timing**: Early rainy season (May-June in CR) **Method**: - Dig hole 30-50
  cm deep - Insert stake at slight angle - Pack soil firmly around base - Do NOT
  water excessively **Success Rate**: 70-90% when done properly
</AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Care and Development">
    **First Year**: - Roots develop within weeks - New shoots appear within
    months - Protect from livestock damage **Ongoing**: - Minimal care once
    established - Pollarding encourages density - First fruit in 3-5 years
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

### Growing from Seed

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Seed Processing">
    **Collection**: - Gather ripe yellow fruits - Remove flesh (soak in water) -
    Clean fibrous pit - Dry in shade **Germination Challenge**: - Seeds have
    hard coat - Germination can take 2-6 months - Scarification helps
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Why Cuttings Are Preferred">

## Identification Guide

### How to Identify Jobo

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Key Features" defaultOpen={true}>
    **Year-Round Identifiers:**

    1. **Deeply furrowed bark** (thick, corky)
    2. **Compound leaves with 9-19 leaflets**
    3. **Aromatic crushed leaves**
    4. **Resinous inner bark**
    5. **Often in living fences**

    **Seasonal Identifiers:**
    - Yellow plum-like fruits (dry season)
    - Deciduous (bare in dry season)
    - Cream flower clusters (before leaves)

  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Distinguishing from Relatives">
