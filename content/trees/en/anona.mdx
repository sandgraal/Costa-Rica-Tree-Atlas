<Callout type="success" title="The Underappreciated Custard Apple">

  Bullock's Heart, is the lesser-known sibling in the famous Annona family that
  includes guanÃ¡bana and chirimoya. Native to the Caribbean and Central America,

  caramelâ€”smoother than guanÃ¡bana but less famous internationally. In Costa

  seek it out in local markets during its season.

  In Costa Rica, "anona" can refer to several related species. *Annona
  reticulata* (this species) is the true Custard Apple, but the name is
  sometimes applied broadly to any Annona with sweet flesh. Always check the
  fruit shape and skin pattern for identification.

## Physical Description

### General Form

The Anona is a small to medium deciduous or semi-deciduous tree with an open, spreading canopy. It loses its leaves briefly during the dry season, with new growth and flowers emerging as the wet season approaches.

#### Bark and Trunk

- **Bark color**: Gray-brown
- **Texture**: Smooth, becoming slightly fissured

- **Type**: Simple, alternate

#### Flowers

- **Location**: On branches, solitary or small clusters
- **Size**: 2-3 cm long
- **Color**: Yellowish-green, fleshy
- **Structure**: 3 outer petals, 3 inner smaller petals
- **Fragrance**: Mild, sweet
- **Timing**: As new leaves emerge

#### Fruit (KEY IDENTIFIER)

- **Shape**: Heart-shaped (cordate)
- **Size**: 8-15 cm diameter
- **Skin**: Smooth to reticulated (netted pattern)
- **Color**: Yellow-brown to reddish when ripe
- **Flesh**: White to cream, custard-like
- **Seeds**: Black, numerous, embedded in flesh
- **Taste**: Sweet, vanilla/caramel notes

</Column>

## Cultivation

### Growing Anona

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Site Requirements">
    **Ideal Conditions**:
    - Full sun to light shade
    - Well-drained soil essential
    - Protection from strong winds
    - Space 5-6 meters apart
    - Lowland locations preferred

    **Considerations**:
    - Tolerates heat well
    - Semi-deciduous appearance
    - Needs dry period to fruit well
    - Cross-pollination improves yields

  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Propagation">
    **From Seed**:
    - Fresh seeds germinate best
    - Scarify or soak seeds 24-48 hours
    - Plant 2-3 cm deep
    - Germination in 2-4 weeks
    - Fruit in 3-5 years

    **Grafting**:
    - For superior varieties
    - Maintains fruit quality
    - Earlier fruiting
    - Use seedling rootstock

    **Air Layering**:
    - Possible but less common
    - Maintains parent characteristics

  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Care">
    **Watering**: Regular when young, reduce when established

    **Fertilization**: Balanced NPK 2-3 times yearly

    **Pruning**: Shape when young, remove dead wood

    **Pollination**: Hand pollination recommended for better yields

    **Harvest**: When fruit gives to gentle pressure and color changes

  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>
