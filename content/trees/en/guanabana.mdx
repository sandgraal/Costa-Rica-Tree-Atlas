## Physical Description

### General Form

The Guan√°bana is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a spreading canopy. It has large, glossy leaves and produces its spectacular fruits directly on the trunk and older branches. The tree is relatively compact, making it ideal for home gardens.

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Bark and Trunk

- **Bark color**: Grayish-brown
- **Texture**: Smooth when young, slightly rough with age
- **Branching**: Low, spreading habit
- **Form**: Often multi-stemmed

#### Leaves

- **Type**: Simple, alternate
- **Shape**: Oblong to oval
- **Size**: 6-18 cm long
- **Texture**: Glossy, leathery
- **Color**: Dark green above, lighter beneath
- **Scent**: Slightly aromatic when crushed

#### Flowers

- **Location**: On trunk and branches (cauliflorous)
- **Size**: 3-5 cm diameter
- **Color**: Yellow-green, fleshy petals
- **Structure**: 6 thick, triangular petals in 2 whorls
- **Fragrance**: Sweet, slightly musky
- **Timing**: Throughout year, peak in dry season

#### Fruit

- **Shape**: Heart-shaped to oval, irregular
- **Size**: 15-35 cm long, weighing 1-7 kg
- **Skin**: Green with soft, curved spines
- **Flesh**: Creamy white, fibrous
- **Seeds**: Black, smooth, numerous

</Column>

<Callout type="tip" title="Recognizing Ripe Fruit">
  A ripe guan√°bana will be: - **Slightly soft** to gentle pressure (like a ripe
  avocado) - **Yellowish-green** rather than dark green - **Fragrant** with a
  sweet, tropical aroma - **Easily yielding** spines that are soft, not rigid Do
  not wait until very soft‚Äîit may be overripe and fermented.

## Cultivation

### Growing Guan√°bana

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Site Selection">
    **Ideal Conditions**:
    - Full sun to partial shade
    - Protected from strong winds
    - Well-drained, fertile soil
    - pH 5.5-6.5
    - Space 6-8 meters between trees

    **Considerations**:
    - Avoid frost-prone areas
    - Needs consistent moisture
    - Low elevation preferred
    - Good air circulation reduces disease

  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Propagation">
    **From Seed** (most common):
    - Use fresh seeds (viability decreases quickly)
    - Remove seed coat or scarify
    - Plant 2-3 cm deep in moist medium
    - Germination in 2-4 weeks
    - Variable fruit quality

    **From Grafting** (recommended):
    - Ensures fruit quality
    - Earlier fruiting (2-3 years vs 4-5)
    - Use seedling rootstock
    - Cleft or veneer grafts work well

  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Care and Maintenance">
    **Watering**: Regular during establishment, moderate once mature

    **Fertilization**: Balanced NPK every 3-4 months

    **Pruning**: Light pruning to maintain shape and remove dead wood

    **Pollination**: Hand pollination often increases yields

    **Pest management**: Monitor for fruit flies and mealybugs

    **Harvest**: Pick when slightly soft, allow to ripen off tree

  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>
