<Callout type="success" title="Nature's Bowl Tree">

  civilizations from the Maya to today's rural communities. This small but

  form, strange trunk-borne flowers, and the signature spherical gourds that
  give it purpose and name. In Costa Rica's Guanacaste, the J√≠caro remains a
  living connection to traditional ways, still providing craft material and
  medicine as it has for thousands of years.

### General Form

The J√≠caro is a small, distinctive tree with a short trunk and spreading, often tortuous branches. Its open, irregular crown gives it a characteristic silhouette, especially when laden with its spherical fruits hanging from trunk and branches.

### Identification Features

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Bark and Trunk

- **Bark color**: Grayish-brown
- **Texture**: Rough, fissured
- **Trunk**: Short, often crooked
- **Branches**: Spreading, twisted
- **Overall**: Distinctive spreading form

#### Leaves

- **Type**: Compound (trifoliate or 3-parted)
- **Leaf stalk**: WINGED (key identifier!)
- **Leaflets**: Obovate, 4-10 cm
- **Texture**: Leathery
- **Arrangement**: Clustered at branch tips
- **Deciduous**: Drops leaves in dry season

</Column>
<Column>

#### Flowers

- **Location**: On trunk and branches (cauliflorous!)
- **Size**: 5-7 cm long
- **Shape**: Bell-shaped, 2-lipped
- **Color**: Yellowish-green with purple veins
- **Odor**: Musky, bat-attracting
- **Timing**: Dry season mainly

#### Fruit (The J√≠cara!)

- **Shape**: Spherical to oval
- **Size**: 8-15 cm diameter
- **Shell**: Hard, woody when dry
- **Color**: Green, turning brown
- **Contents**: Pulp with many seeds
- **Persistence**: Long time on tree

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

<Callout type="tip" title="The Winged Leaves">
  The easiest way to confirm Crescentia alata: **Look at the Leaf Stalk**: -
  Stalk has flat "wings" running along it - Makes the stalk look flattened -
  Connects 3 leaflets (trifoliate) - WINGED = "alata" in the scientific name
  **Compare to C. cujete**: - C. cujete has simple, unwinged leaves - Leaves
  grouped but not compound - Stalks round, not winged This single feature
  separates the two species!
</Callout>

## Ecological Importance

### Pollination

<Callout type="success" title="Bat Pollination">
  The J√≠caro's strange flowers are perfectly designed for bats: **Flower
  Adaptations**: - Open at night - Musky, fermented odor - Pale/greenish color -
  Large, sturdy structure - Produced on trunk (accessible) **Main Pollinators**:
  - Nectar-feeding bats - Especially leaf-nosed bats - Also some moths **The
  Relationship**: - Bats visit for nectar - Pollen on face transferred - Tree
  gets cross-pollination - Bats get food resource **Conservation Implication**:
  - J√≠caro depends on healthy bat populations - Loss of bats = less fruit
  production - Tree supports bat populations - Mutual dependency
</Callout>

## Cultivation

### Growing J√≠caro

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Site Requirements">
    **Ideal Conditions**: - Full sun essential - Dry tropical climate -
    Well-drained soils - Tolerates poor, rocky soils - Distinct dry season **Not
    Suitable For**: - Wet climates (too humid) - Heavy shade - Waterlogged soils
    - Cold temperatures
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Propagation">
    **From Seed** (most common): - Extract seeds from ripe fruit - Clean and dry
    - Sow fresh for best results - Germination: 2-4 weeks - Slow initial growth
    **First Fruiting**: - May take 5-8 years from seed - Patience required! -
    Trees are long-lived once established
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Care">
    **Young Trees**: - Protect from cattle - Water if needed first year -
    Minimal pruning needed **Mature Trees**: - Very drought tolerant - Almost no
    maintenance - May prune for access - Long-lived with minimal care
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>

## Identification Guide

### How to Identify J√≠caro

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Key Identification Features">
    **Primary Identifiers:** 1. **Spherical gourd fruits** on trunk/branches 2.
    **Winged leaf stalks** (C. alata) 3. **Trifoliate leaves** (3 leaflets) 4.
    **Open, spreading crown** 5. **Flowers directly on trunk** (cauliflory)
    **Supporting Features**: - Small tree (under 12 m) - Dry forest/savanna
    habitat - Deciduous in dry season - Guanacaste region primarily
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Distinguishing from C. cujete">
    | Feature | C. alata (This species) | C. cujete |

    side | Wider | The winged leaf stalks are the definitive identifier for C.
