<AccordionItem title="ðŸ”¬ Taxonomic Notes">

Surprisingly, teak belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), not a typical timber tree family. The genus _Tectona_ contains only 3 species, with _T. grandis_ being by far the most important commercially.

**Origins**: Teak is native to India, Myanmar, Thailand, and Laos. It was introduced to Costa Rica in the 1920s and has since become one of the country's most important plantation species.

**Plantation Industry**: Costa Rica is now one of the world's leading teak producers, with over 60,000 hectares under cultivation, primarily in Guanacaste and the Northern Zone.
