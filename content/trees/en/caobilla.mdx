# Caobilla

<Callout type="success" title="The Medicine Tree of the Rainforest">
  **Caobilla** (_Carapa guianensis_), also known as **Andiroba** or
  **Crabwood**, is a remarkable rainforest tree prized for both its
  mahogany-like timber and its oil-rich seeds. Indigenous peoples have extracted
  medicinal oil from its nuts for centuries, while its durable wood remains
  highly valued for construction and furniture.
</Callout>

<AccordionItem title="ðŸ”¬ Taxonomic Notes">

_Carapa_ is a small genus of about 10 species in the mahogany family (Meliaceae). The species epithet _guianensis_ refers to the Guiana region of South America where it was first scientifically described.

**Related Species**: _C. procera_ occurs in West Africa (remarkable transatlantic distribution). In Costa Rica, Caobilla is related to true Mahogany (_Swietenia_) and Cedar (_Cedrela_).

**Oil Quality**: The seed oil (Andiroba oil) is one of the most valued non-timber forest products from Amazonian forests, rivaling Brazil nut oil in economic importance.
