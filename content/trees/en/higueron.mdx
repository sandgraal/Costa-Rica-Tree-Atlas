## Physical Description

### Overall Form

The Higuer√≥n is a massive tree that can begin life as an epiphyte (strangler fig) or grow from ground level (hemi-epiphyte). It develops a spreading crown, massive trunk often with fused aerial roots, and produces abundant latex when cut.

### Distinctive Features

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Trunk & Structure

- **Trunk**: Massive, often irregularly shaped
- **Aerial Roots**: Dramatic, may fuse together
- **Buttresses**: Spreading, web-like
- **Growth Habit**: Strangler or free-standing
- **Latex**: Abundant white latex when cut

#### Leaves

- **Type**: Simple, alternate
- **Shape**: Elliptic to oblong
- **Size**: 10-25 cm long, 5-10 cm wide
- **Texture**: Leathery, glossy
- **Margin**: Entire
- **Stipules**: Prominent at growing tips

</Column>
<Column>

#### Figs (Syconia)

- **Type**: Syconium (inverted flower cluster)
- **Size**: 2-4 cm diameter
- **Color**: Green to yellowish-green when ripe
- **Position**: In leaf axils along branches
- **Season**: Year-round (asynchronous)
- **Taste**: Insipid (species name reference)

#### Reproduction

- **Pollination**: Specific fig wasp (_Tetrapus_ species)
- **Dispersal**: Birds, bats, primates, fish, tapirs
- **Germination**: Often in tree crowns (epiphytic start)
- **Strategy**: Can strand host tree as strangler

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

<Callout type="tip" title="The Strangler Lifestyle">
  Many Higuer√≥n trees begin life when a bird or bat deposits a seed in another
  tree's canopy. The fig germinates there, sending roots down the host trunk
  while growing toward the sunlight. Over decades, these roots fuse around the
  host, eventually killing it and leaving the fig standing as a hollow giant‚Äî
  hence the name "strangler fig." However, many also grow directly from the
  ground.
</Callout>

## Ecological Significance

### The Keystone Species

<Callout type="success" title="Feeding the Forest">
  Figs like the Higuer√≥n are considered **keystone species**‚Äîorganisms whose
  impact on their ecosystem is disproportionately large relative to their
  abundance. A single Higuer√≥n can produce **one million or more figs per
  year**, and because different individual trees fruit at different times, there
  is almost always a Higuer√≥n fruiting somewhere in the forest. This year-round
  fruit availability makes them critical for wildlife survival.
</Callout>

### Conservation Importance

<Callout type="info" title="Protecting the Keystones">
  While the Higuer√≥n itself is not threatened, its conservation importance
  cannot be overstated: - **Protecting Higuerones protects ecosystems**: The
  wildlife dependent on figs represents a large portion of tropical biodiversity
  - **Fragmentation impact**: Isolated Higuerones may lose their wasp

## Cultivation & Propagation

### Growing Higuer√≥n

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Propagation Methods

**From Seeds**

1. Extract seeds from ripe figs
2. Seeds are tiny‚Äîmix with sand to sow
3. Surface sow (need light)
4. Keep consistently moist
5. Germination in 2-4 weeks
6. Very slow initial growth

**From Cuttings (Easier)**

1. Large branch cuttings (30-60 cm)
2. Can be planted directly as living posts
3. Root quickly in wet season
4. Much faster establishment than seed
