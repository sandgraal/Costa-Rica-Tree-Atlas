<AccordionItem title="üî¨ Taxonomic Notes">

_Artocarpus_ means "bread fruit" in Greek (artos = bread, karpos = fruit). The species epithet _altilis_ means "fat" or "nourishing," referring to the nutritious fruit.

**Relation to Jackfruit**: Breadfruit is closely related to jackfruit (_Artocarpus heterophyllus_), and both belong to the same genus. Breadfruit is smaller and seedless in most cultivated varieties.

**Historical Introduction**: British Captain William Bligh of the HMS Bounty was famously tasked with introducing breadfruit to the Caribbean as cheap food for enslaved workers.

</AccordionItem>

<Callout type="info" title="The Bounty Connection">
  Breadfruit's arrival in the Caribbean is tied to the infamous HMS Bounty
  mutiny of 1789. Captain William Bligh was transporting breadfruit plants from
  Tahiti to Jamaica when the crew mutinied. Bligh later succeeded on a second
  voyage, establishing breadfruit throughout the Caribbean.
</Callout>
