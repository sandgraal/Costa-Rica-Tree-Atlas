## Physical Description

### Overall Form

Por√≥ Gigante is a tall, fast-growing tree with a broad, spreading crown that provides excellent shade coverage. The trunk often develops buttresses at the base and may have scattered thorns when young. During the dry season, it loses its leaves and produces masses of brilliant orange-red flowers‚Äîa spectacular sight across coffee-growing regions.

### The Perfect Shade Tree

<Callout type="success" title="Sustainable Partnership">

## Nitrogen Fixation

### Natural Fertilizer Factory

<Callout type="info" title="Symbiotic Relationship">
  Like other legumes, Por√≥ Gigante hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria (Rhizobium)
  in nodules on its roots. These bacteria convert atmospheric nitrogen into
  forms plants can use. A single Por√≥ tree can add 30-60 kg of nitrogen per
  hectare annually‚Äîequivalent to significant fertilizer inputs.
</Callout>

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### How It Works

1. Rhizobium bacteria enter root hairs
2. Form specialized nodules
3. Bacteria fix atmospheric N‚ÇÇ
4. Convert to ammonia (NH‚ÇÉ)
5. Tree shares sugars with bacteria
6. Nitrogen enriches surrounding soil

</Column>
<Column>

#### Ecosystem Benefits

- Reduces need for synthetic fertilizers
- Improves soil structure
- Supports soil microbiome
- Benefits neighboring plants
- Sustainable long-term fertility
- Climate-friendly agriculture

</Column>
</TwoColumn>
