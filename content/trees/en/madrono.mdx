### General Form

The Madro√±o is a medium-sized tree with a rounded to irregular crown. Its smooth, grayish bark and relatively small leaves give way during flowering season to an explosion of white that completely transforms the tree's appearance.

### Identification Features

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Bark and Trunk

- **Bark color**: Grayish to light brown
- **Texture**: Smooth to slightly fissured
- **Pattern**: May peel in thin strips
- **Trunk**: Usually single, straight
- **Inner bark**: Yellowish (hence "lemonwood")

#### Leaves

- **Type**: Simple, opposite
- **Shape**: Elliptic to oblong
- **Size**: 6-15 cm long
- **Texture**: Leathery, glossy
- **Color**: Dark green above, paler below
- **Margin**: Entire (smooth edge)

</Column>
<Column>

#### Flowers

- **Size**: Small (5-8 mm)
- **Color**: Pure white
- **Arrangement**: Dense terminal clusters
- **Fragrance**: Sweet, honey-like
- **Timing**: Dry season (January-April)
- **Coverage**: COMPLETE - entire crown white!

#### Fruit

- **Type**: Small capsule
- **Size**: 5-8 mm
- **Seeds**: Tiny, wind-dispersed
- **Timing**: Following flowering
- **Appearance**: Inconspicuous

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

## Cultivation

### Growing Madro√±o

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Site Requirements">
    **Ideal Conditions**: - Full sun - Well-drained soils - Tropical dry forest
    climate - Distinct dry season (for flowering) - Frost-free **Soil
    Preferences**: - Tolerates poor soils - Good drainage essential - Neutral to
    slightly alkaline - Adapted to seasonal drought
  </AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="Propagation">
  **From Seed** (most common): - Collect from mature capsules - Sow fresh (short
  viability) - Light required for germination - Germination: 2-4 weeks **From
  Cuttings**: - Possible but less common - Large hardwood cuttings - Living
  fence posts may root **Growth Rate**: - Moderate to slow - First flowers: 5-10
  years - Reaches full size: 30-50 years
</AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Care and Maintenance">

    Minimal pruning needed - Fertilizer not required **Mature Trees**: - Very
    drought tolerant - Little maintenance needed - Prune only for shape -
    Long-lived with minimal care
  </AccordionItem>
