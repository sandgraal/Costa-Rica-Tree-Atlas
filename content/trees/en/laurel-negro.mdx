# Laurel Negro

<Callout type="success" title="Costa Rica's Black Gold">
  **Laurel Negro** (_Cordia megalantha_), also known as **Black Laurel**, is one
  of the most prized native timber trees of Costa Rica's Pacific slope. Its
  dense, dark heartwood produces furniture and construction timber of
  exceptional quality, comparable to imported precious woods.

<AccordionItem title="ðŸ”¬ Taxonomic Notes">

_Cordia_ is a large pantropical genus with about 300 species. The name _megalantha_ means "large-flowered" in Greek (mega = large, anthos = flower), referring to its showy white flowers.

**Related Species**: The more common _C. alliodora_ (Laurel) produces lighter wood. _C. gerascanthus_ (Spanish Elm) is another valuable Central American timber species. Laurel Negro's darker heartwood distinguishes it from these relatives.

**Wood Distinction**: Unlike the common Laurel (_C. alliodora_) which has pale heartwood, Laurel Negro develops distinctively dark, dense timber.

</AccordionItem>
