## Physical Description

### General Form

The Cipr√©s is a medium to tall conifer with a distinctively narrow, columnar to pyramidal crown. Its dense, dark green foliage and straight trunk make it instantly recognizable. In plantations, trees are often grown close together, creating characteristic dark walls of green.

### Identification Features

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Bark and Trunk

- **Bark color**: Gray to reddish-brown
- **Texture**: Fissured, peeling in strips when mature
- **Pattern**: Vertical ridges and furrows
- **Trunk**: Straight, single main stem
- **Aromatic**: Distinct cypress scent when cut

#### Foliage

- **Type**: Scale-like leaves (not needles)
- **Arrangement**: Overlapping, pressed to twigs
- **Color**: Dark green to blue-green
- **Texture**: Soft to touch (vs. prickly juniper)
- **Aromatic**: Crush for cypress scent

</Column>
<Column>

#### Reproductive Structures

**Seed Cones**:

- Woody, spherical
- Size: 1-2 cm diameter
- 6-8 scales per cone
- Gray-brown when mature
- Persist on tree

**Pollen Cones**:

- Small, at branch tips
- Yellow when releasing pollen
- Late dry season

#### Crown Shape

- **Young trees**: Narrow pyramidal
- **Mature**: Columnar to conical
- **Dense**: Light-blocking foliage
- **Single leader**: Central trunk dominant

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

<Callout type="tip" title="Cypress vs. Pine Identification">
  Distinguishing Cipr√©s from pines (which also grow in Costa Rica highlands): |

## Cultivation

### Growing Cipr√©s

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Site Requirements">
    **Ideal Conditions**: - Highland climate (1,200-2,800 m elevation) - Cool
    temperatures, frost-tolerant - Full sun essential - Well-drained soils -
    1,000-2,500 mm rainfall **Site Preparation**: - Clear competing vegetation -
    Prepare planting holes - No waterlogged sites - Wind protection for young
    trees helpful
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Propagation">
    **From Seed** (most common): - Collect from mature cones - Cold
    stratification helpful - Sow in nursery beds - Transplant at 30-50 cm height
    - High germination rates **From Cuttings**: - Possible but less common -
    Semi-hardwood cuttings - Rooting hormone beneficial - Used for selected
    clones
  </AccordionItem>

  <AccordionItem title="Plantation Management">
    **Spacing**: - Timber: 3 x 3 m (1,100/ha) - Windbreaks: 2 m between trees -
    Living fence: 2-3 m spacing **Care**: - Weed control first 2-3 years -
    Pruning for clear trunk (timber) - Thinning at 8-10 years - Fire protection
    important **Harvest**: - First thinning: 10-12 years - Final harvest: 18-25
    years - Can coppice (resprout) poorly - Usually replanted
  </AccordionItem>
</Accordion>
