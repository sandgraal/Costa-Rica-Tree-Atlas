## The Famous Red Sap

### Dragon's Blood Chemistry

<Callout type="warning" title="Medicinal Marvel">
  The red sap of Targu√° contains a complex mixture of compounds including
  proanthocyanidins, diterpenes, and alkaloids. Scientific studies have
  confirmed antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and wound-healing properties,
  validating centuries of traditional use.
</Callout>

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Active Compounds

- **Taspine**: Wound healing alkaloid
- **Proanthocyanidins**: Antioxidants
- **Lignans**: Anti-inflammatory
- **Diterpenes**: Antimicrobial
- **Catechins**: Various properties

</Column>
<Column>

#### Traditional Uses

- Wound healing (direct application)
- Mouth sores and gum disease
- Stomach ulcers (internal)
- Skin infections
- Post-childbirth healing
- Insect bites

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

### How to Collect Sap

Traditional harvesting method:

1. Make a shallow cut in the bark (not too deep)
2. Red sap flows immediately
3. Collect in clean container
4. Allow tree to heal between harvests
5. Sustainable harvesting doesn't harm the tree

<Callout type="info">
  The sap coagulates quickly when exposed to air, forming a protective "scab"
  similar to how blood clots, adding to its wound-healing reputation.
</Callout>

## Interesting Facts

<Accordion>
<AccordionItem title="ü©∏ Blood Mimic">

The red sap of Targu√° not only looks like blood but also acts like it in some
ways. When exposed to air, it quickly coagulates and forms a protective barrier,
similar to how blood clots. This natural "bandage" effect is one reason it's so
effective for wound healing‚Äîit literally seals cuts while delivering beneficial
compounds.

</AccordionItem>
<AccordionItem title="üåø Living Pharmacy">

Indigenous peoples throughout Central and South America have used dragon's blood
trees as living pharmacies for millennia. The sap was so valued that during
colonial times it was traded internationally and even used in European medicine.
Modern science has validated many traditional uses, finding genuine antimicrobial
and wound-healing properties.

</AccordionItem>
<AccordionItem title="üöÄ Explosive Seeds">

When Targu√° seed capsules dry out, they split open violently, literally exploding
and launching seeds several meters from the parent tree. This ballistic dispersal
is supplemented by birds eating any remaining seeds, ensuring the pioneer species
can quickly colonize distant disturbed areas.

</AccordionItem>
<AccordionItem title="üå°Ô∏è Climate Hero">

As a fast-growing pioneer species, Targu√° captures carbon dioxide rapidly during
its short life. In areas recovering from deforestation, stands of Targu√° can
sequester significant carbon while preparing the site for longer-lived forest
trees. This makes it valuable for both restoration and climate mitigation.

</AccordionItem>
