# Orey

<Callout type="success" title="Master of the Swamps">
  **Orey** (_Campnosperma panamense_) is a remarkable wetland specialist that
  dominates the flooded forests of Costa Rica's Caribbean lowlands. With its
  distinctive stilt roots and ability to thrive in permanently waterlogged
  conditions, it represents one of nature's most specialized tree adaptations.
</Callout>

<AccordionItem title="ðŸ”¬ Taxonomic Notes">

_Campnosperma_ is a small genus of about 10 species, mostly found in Southeast Asia and the Pacific, with _C. panamense_ being the only American representative. The genus name means "curved seed" (Greek: kamptos = curved, sperma = seed).

**Family Connection**: As a member of Anacardiaceae, Orey is related to mangoes, cashews, and pistachios, though it shares none of their edible qualities.

**Unique Distribution**: The presence of this genus in the Americas represents a fascinating biogeographic puzzle, suggesting ancient connections across continents.

</AccordionItem>
