## Physical Description

### General Form

The Guapinol is a large, semi-deciduous to evergreen tree with a massive trunk and broad spreading crown. It's one of the emergent giants of Neotropical forests, often towering above the surrounding canopy. The bark exudes fragrant amber-colored resin, and the seed pods are unmistakableâ€”both visually and olfactorily!

### Distinctive Features

<TwoColumn>
<Column>

#### Leaves

- **Type**: Compound, 2 leaflets
- **Shape**: Asymmetrical, sickle-shaped
- **Size**: 5-10 cm each leaflet
- **Texture**: Leathery, glossy
- **Feature**: Oil glands visible as dots
- **Color**: Dark green above, paler below

#### Bark and Resin

- **Bark**: Gray-brown, smooth becoming scaly
- **Resin**: Clear to amber, aromatic
- **Location**: Exudes from trunk wounds
- **Use**: Becomes copal/amber when hardened
- **Scent**: Aromatic, like incense

</Column>
<Column>

#### The Infamous Fruit

- **Type**: Woody legume pod
- **Shape**: Oblong, like a fat toe
- **Size**: 10-18 cm long, 5-7 cm wide
- **Shell**: Very hard, woody
- **Pulp**: Dry, powdery, cream to tan
- **Seeds**: 2-5 large seeds in pulp
- **Smell**: Like stinky cheese or feet!

#### Flowers

- **Type**: White to cream, fragrant
- **Size**: Small, 10-15 mm
- **Arrangement**: Terminal panicles
- **Pollination**: Bats, moths
- **Season**: Usually dry season

</Column>
</TwoColumn>

## The Amber Connection

### Copal and Fossil Amber

<FeatureBox title="From Resin to Amber: A 20-Million-Year Story" icon="ðŸ’Ž">
  The Guapinol genus (_Hymenaea_) is the source of some of the world's most
  famous amber deposits: **Living Resin (Copal)**: - Fresh resin is sticky,
  aromatic - Hardens into "copal" over decades/centuries - Used in varnishes,
  incense, traditional medicine **Fossil Amber**: - Extinct _Hymenaea_ species
  created famous ambers - Dominican amber: 15-20 million years old - Mexican
  amber: 22-26 million years old - Often contains trapped insects, spiders,
  plants - Key source for paleontology research Today's Guapinol is the living
  relative of these amber-producing trees, and its resin will eventually become
  amber in millions of years!
</FeatureBox>

<Callout type="info" title="Jurassic Park Connection">
  The famous movie premise of extracting dinosaur DNA from amber mosquitoes is
  science fiction, but amber DOES preserve remarkable ancient specimens. Most
  famous amber comes from extinct relatives of the Guapinol. The amber you buy
  today may have come from trees very similar to those still standing in Costa
  Rican forests!
</Callout>

- **Primary**: Large rodents (agoutis, pacas)

<Callout type="success" title="A Keystone Species">

## Cultivation and Propagation

### Growing Guapinol

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Seed Collection and Preparation">
    **Collection**: - Collect ripe pods from ground (March-June typically) -
    Look for pods with hard, intact shells - Store in dry conditions
    **Preparation**: - Crack open woody shell (hammer works!) - Extract seeds
    from stinky pulp - Clean seeds thoroughly - **Scarification required**: Nick
    seed coat with file or sandpaper - Or: Soak in hot water 24 hours -
    Germination: 2-6 weeks after treatment
  </AccordionItem>

<AccordionItem title="Growing Conditions">
  **Nursery Stage**: - Use deep containers (taproot develops early) -
  Well-drained potting mix - Full sun to light shade - Regular watering, no
  waterlogging **Transplanting**: - After 6-12 months, when 30-50 cm tall -
  Choose permanent location (long-lived tree!) - Dig deep hole for taproot -
  Water well during first dry season
</AccordionItem>

## Identification Guide

### How to Identify Guapinol

<Accordion>
  <AccordionItem title="Key Identification Features" defaultOpen={true}>
    **Definitive Features:**

    1. **Compound leaves with exactly 2 leaflets**
    2. **Leaflets asymmetrical and sickle-shaped**
    3. **Oil glands visible as dots** (hold leaf to light)
    4. **Large woody "toe-shaped" pods**
    5. **Amber-colored resin** on trunk
    6. **Large spreading crown** in mature trees

  </AccordionItem>
