# Sangrillo

<Callout type="success" title="The Blood Tree of the Swamps">
  **Sangrillo** (_Pterocarpus officinalis_) is one of the most distinctive trees
  of Costa Rica's Caribbean wetlands. Named for its blood-red sap ("sangre"
  means blood in Spanish), this massive tree develops spectacular buttress roots
  that can extend several meters from the trunk.
</Callout>

<AccordionItem title="ðŸ”¬ Taxonomic Notes">

_Pterocarpus_ is a pantropical genus of about 35 species. The genus name means "winged fruit" (Greek: pteron = wing, karpos = fruit), referring to the distinctive winged seed pods. The species epithet _officinalis_ indicates its historical use in medicine.

**Related Species**: _P. rohrii_ (Blood Tree) also occurs in Costa Rica at higher elevations. The famous African Padauk (_P. soyauxii_) and Asian Narra (_P. indicus_) are relatives prized for their wood.

**Nitrogen Fixation**: Like many legumes, Sangrillo fixes atmospheric nitrogen through root nodule bacteria, enriching wetland soils.

</AccordionItem>
