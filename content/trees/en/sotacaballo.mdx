# Sotacaballo (Riverside Mimosa)

<Callout type="success" title="Guardian of the Riverbanks">
  The **Sotacaballo** (_Zygia longifolia_) is one of the most characteristic
  trees of Costa Rica's waterways. Its distinctive habit of leaning dramatically
  over rivers and streams provides essential shade for aquatic life while its
  roots stabilize banks against erosion. The powder-puff flowers are a favorite
  of bees and hummingbirds.
</Callout>

## Physical Description

### Overall Form

The Sotacaballo is a small to medium evergreen tree with a distinctive growth habit. It almost always leans toward water, often at dramatic angles, with its branches sweeping over the river surface. The trunk frequently forks low, creating multiple stems that arch gracefully over waterways.

transition when other food sources are scarce.
